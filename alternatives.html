Good Oils

Despite the unhealthy nature of these processed oils, and the common idea that fats aren't good for you in general, some very much are! Olive oil is an easy to obtain oil that can be found in most households, and is very good for you. Especially your heart. Studies about the Mediterranean diet (the one with the lowest rate of heart disease) found olive oil as a major component of that diet. And further examination of this oil found that it lowers inflammation, improves the lining of your blood vessels, and prevents excessive clotting which are all associated with heart disease. Olive oil has also been linked to reducing the risk of developing Alzheimer's, type 2 diabetes, and even cancer as well as helping to prevent strokes! Also, coconut oil! Coconut oil is a very healthy alternative, containing many necessary fats. It has many uses that can improve cognitive function, metabolism, as well as hair, and skin health. Also, coconut oil! Don't Stress!

The facts related to processed seed oils combined with how widespread they are make it easy to fear them. But in reality, like anything, these oils are fine in moderation. In small amounts, they won't have a significant effect on your health. They simply make things taste amazing. As long as you maintain a healthy diet most of the time, and don't go overboard with these oils, then don't stress.

Conclusion

Seed oils are easy to produce, cheap to buy, convenient to use, and make things taste delicious. But at a hidden cost of being very unhealthy. Now you should be a bit more educated and a bit more aware of what you're eating. But don't worry. As long as you make good decisions most of the time, there's no reason not to enjoy a bag of chips every once in a while!

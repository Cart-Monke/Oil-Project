Oils and our health

The problem with these oils is that the amount we have begun consuming them has skyrocketed since their invention. The ratio of omega-6 fatty acids (present in these oils) to omega-3 fatty acids (found more in fish / nuts / un-processed fats) that the average person consumes has skyrocketed from about 1:1 to 20:1. These disparities in fatty acid type consumption are linked to numerous health effects. This disparity is linked to obesity, heart disease, cardiovascular disease, obesity, inflammatory bowel disease, rheumatoid arthritis, and Alzheimer's disease. Also, the nutrients that are present in unprocessed oils aren't found in these processed oils. The bleaching and deodorization steps of the refining processes remove all impurities in the oil, volatile compounds and good compounds alike. Not to mention the high calorie content of these oils. This in itself isn't the problem. A handful of walnuts has more calories than a tablespoon of these oils. The problem is that they are in everything. It is well known that the U.S is suffering an obesity crisis, and the use of these oils in so many products on our market shelves has invested their fair share into causing this crisis.
